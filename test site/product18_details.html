-Onboard textile

"Onboard textile" refers to the range of textile products and materials used within an aircraft cabin environment. These textiles serve various purposes, including providing comfort, enhancing aesthetics, ensuring safety, and maintaining cleanliness. Common examples of onboard textiles include seat covers, headrest covers, blankets, pillows, curtains, carpets, and even uniforms worn by cabin crew. The selection of onboard textiles is crucial for airlines as they aim to create a comfortable and visually appealing environment for passengers while also meeting safety and regulatory standards. Textiles used in aircraft cabins are often chosen for their durability, ease of maintenance, flame-retardant properties, and resistance to stains and odors. In recent years, there has been a growing focus on sustainability in the aviation industry, leading airlines to explore eco-friendly alternatives for onboard textiles. These may include textiles made from recycled materials, biodegradable fabrics, or those produced using environmentally friendly manufacturing processes. Overall, onboard textiles play a significant role in shaping the passenger experience and contributing to the overall ambiance and functionality of the aircraft cabin.
